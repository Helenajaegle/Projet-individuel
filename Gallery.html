The most important faces of feminism

Simone Veil

1927-2017 born in Nice, is a French stateswoman. She passed the law decriminalizing the use of abortion (Voluntary Interruption of Pregnancy) the «law Veil».She appears as an icon of the fight against gender discrimination.

Frida Kahlo

1907-1954, born in Mexico, was a painter and feminist who fought for the poorest women in her country.

Doria Shafik

born on December 14, 1908 and died on September 20, 1975. She was an Egyptian philosopher, poet and feminist. Fighting for women with the support of many women, she broke into the parliament to make demands related to the social and economic rights of women. She had started a hunger strike to be able to give a conference on Egyptian women.

Huda Sharawi

was born on June 23, 1879 and died on December 12, 1947. She was qualified as a precursor of feminism not only in Egypt but also in the Arab feminist movement. She will create a school to give courses to women of domestic hygiene and childcare. Then she will create an association to help poor young women to have access to education.

Mona Eltahawy

was born on August 1, 1967, she has dual Egyptian-American citizenship. She is a writer and feminist. In her speeches, she shows that women should not let men do it to them. On the contrary, she tries to make women afraid of the patriarchy.

Olympe de Gouges

1748-1793, born in Montauban, was recognized as a pioneer of French feminism. She was the author of the "Declaration of the Rights of Women and Citizens".

Elizabeth Cady Starton

was born on November 12, 1815 and died on October 26, 1902. She was an American citizen and was a writer, surfer and feminist. She tackled the issue of fair divorce and then fought for women's right to vote.
